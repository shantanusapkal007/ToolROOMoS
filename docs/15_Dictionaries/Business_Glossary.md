# Business Glossary
*ToolRoomOS – Manufacturing Operating System*

## Purpose
To eliminate communication errors between developers, management, and shop floor operators, this glossary defines the precise meaning of every manufacturing term used within ToolRoomOS. 

If a term is not in this document, it does not exist in the software.

---

## 1. Core Concepts
- **Project:** The root entity of all work. A Project represents a single customer order to manufacture a specific part, die, or mold. Everything revolves around the Project.
- **Workflow:** The physical and digital sequence of stages a Project must follow (Engineering -> Purchase -> GRN -> Inventory -> Production -> Quality -> Dispatch -> Finance).
- **Master Data:** The permanent, reusable data that defines the factory (e.g., Materials, Customers, Machines, Employees).
- **Business Event:** An immutable action recorded in the database that signifies a physical action occurred in the factory (e.g., "Material Received", "Machine Started").

## 2. Documents & Requirements
- **Customer PO (Purchase Order):** The official order from the customer authorizing the toolroom to begin work and agreeing to pay a specific amount.
- **Drawing:** The CAD file (DXF/STEP/PDF) provided by the customer or generated by engineering detailing the exact physical dimensions and tolerances of the part.
- **BOM (Bill of Materials):** The comprehensive list of all raw materials, standard parts, and hardware required to manufacture the project.
- **Routing:** The step-by-step sequence of machine operations (e.g., Milling -> Drilling -> Heat Treatment -> Grinding) required to turn the raw material into the finished drawing.

## 3. Procurement & Inventory
- **Material Requirement:** The exact quantity and grade of material the engineering department states must be purchased.
- **Vendor PO (Purchase Order):** The official document sent to a supplier to purchase raw materials or subcontracted services.
- **GRN (Goods Receipt Note):** The document generated when physical material arrives at the factory gate. Creating a GRN converts Planned Cost into Actual Cost.
- **Batch:** A specific, traceable lot of material received under a single GRN. 
- **Material Issue:** The physical transfer of material from the warehouse (Stores) to the shop floor (Production) specifically assigned to a Project.

## 4. Production & Quality
- **Operation:** A single step in the Routing (e.g., "CNC Milling").
- **Machine Shop Daily Report (MSDR):** The daily log filled out by an operator detailing which machine they ran, for what project, and for how many hours.
- **Spindle Time / Cutting Time:** The actual time a machine spends cutting metal (Productive Time).
- **Setup Time:** The time spent preparing the machine and fixtures before cutting begins (Non-Productive Time).
- **Inspection Report:** A dimensional check of a part against the Engineering Drawing tolerances.
- **NCR (Non-Conformance Report):** A formal document generated when a part fails inspection, detailing the defect and requiring a decision (Scrap or Rework).
- **Scrap:** Material that is damaged beyond repair and must be thrown away.
- **Rework:** A part that failed inspection but can be fixed by performing additional machine operations.

## 5. Logistics & Finance
- **Dispatch Note (Delivery Challan):** The document accompanying the finished goods on the truck to the customer.
- **Tax Invoice:** The final financial document sent to the customer requesting payment. Generates Revenue.
- **Estimated Cost:** The theoretical cost calculated during the quoting phase.
- **Actual Cost:** The real, accumulated cost of the physical material and actual machine hours spent on the project.
- **Profitability:** Actual Revenue (from Invoice) minus Total Actual Cost.
